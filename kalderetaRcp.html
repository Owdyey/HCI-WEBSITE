Kaldereta

Caldereta's name is derived from the Spanish word caldera meaning cauldron. The dish is similar to meat stews from the Iberian Peninsula and was brought to the Philippines by the Spaniards during their 300-year occupation of the Philippines.

How to cook?

Ingredients

  • 1 kg. beef brisket
  • Â¼ pc. onion chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic minced
  • 2 1/2 cups water
  • 2 Tbsp. liver spread
  • 1 (45g) pack Knorr Complete Recipe Mix Kaldereta dissolved in 2 1/2cups water
  • 1 pc. red bell pepper
  • 1 green bell pepper diced
  • 1 piece potato diced
  • 1 pc carrot quartered
  • 1 cup green peas
  • 1 piece siling labuyo

Procedure

  • Step 1:

    Let's begin by frying the beef in a hot pot with a little bit of oil. Set this aside then throw in the garlic and onions. Return the fried meat back to the pot and add in the water. Bring to a boil, then simmer until meat becomes tender.

  • Step 2:

    Mix in liver spread and Knorr Complete Recipe Matrix Kaldereta (mixed in water). Boil for 2 minutes.

  • Step 3:

    Add in vegetables and simmer until sauce thickens. You can now enjoy Kaldereta any time you please. With Knorr Kaldereta Complete Recipe Mix, cooking becomes more easy.
